Access

Importance

A week-long informational series that educates the UC community on the importance and advantages of open educational resources and encourages faculty to incorporate them into their academic instruction to increase affordability for students.

Impact

This initiative will impact faculty and students with a focus on increasing affordability and accessibility of academic resources.

Implementation

Working internally with the Student Rights & Interest Committee and externally with Faculty Senate, we would host a week of events surrounding academic affordability and provide free resources and discounts to students. In addition, there will be an advocacy component that urges faculty to incorporate open educational resources into their courses.

Importance

Stay informed and gain a broader perspective inside and outside of classrooms with news subscriptions to media publications such as The Wall Street Journal, New York Times, and more at no cost.

>

Impact

This initiative would impact all students by increasing access to news resources that can be utilized for academic studies and research. Secondly, increasing access to these resources encourages students to stay informed on current events and promote civic engagement in university related issues.

Implementation

It's important that we are continuously looking at incremental changes that can increase affordability and access to different resources that impact academia. We aim to conduct a survey to ensure we provide resources we know our students will be inclined to utilize as well as see the various ways in which these resources would impact our community. Following these efforts, we will establish funding for the university subscription to these resources and working with various offices to promote their access.

Importance

Provide student-run review sessions for MCAT, LSAT, and GRE each semester.

Impact

This initiative will affect all students by providing the opportunity to free resources and collaborative peer review for common standardize testing for graduate studies,

Implementation

The goal of this initiative is too increase accessibility and affordability associated with pursuing post baccalaureate degrees.

Through working with various undergraduate and graduate program offices, we will compile a list of quality resources to purchase for students who agree to be apart of the session. We will host the program each semester in collaboration with faculty and graduate students for those interested.

Importance

Working with the Counseling & Psychological Services (CAPS) and the Student Wellness Center, we will create a continuous supply of grab-and-go self-wellness kits to distribute across all campuses

Impact

This initiative will impact students by increasing mental health resource availability and encouraging self-care throughout the semester rather than during stress points in academia.

Implementation

Through internal efforts from our Director of Mental Health Resources and external efforts with CAPS and the Student Wellness Center, we will finalize the content for the kits and distribute them to embedded counselors, identity offices, resident halls, and across our campuses.

Inclusion

Importance

Creating an exhibit that showcases the history, legacy, and beauty of our ethnic and cultural identities as an addition to the upcoming installation of the Identity and Inclusion Center in the Steger Student Life Center.

Impact

This initiative will impact all students with a focus on uplifting and celebrating the identities and legacies of our marginalized communities. Furthermore, this initiative surrounds supporting the development of the Identity and Inclusion Center, a space with the purpose of further empowering our various identities beyond our current identity-based centers.

Implementation

This initiative will center student input and promote collaboration through extensive research within our ethnic & cultural student organizations, identity offices, and UC historian. Next, the information from this research will be use to create a mockup for the exhibit to be included in Planning, Design and Construction’s building plans for the space.

Importance

Increase unity among all campuses through monthly meetings between the regional presidency, semester townhalls, and developed social spaces for our regional campuses.

>

Impact

Through increasing unity between Uptown, UC Blue Ash, and Clermont, this initiative will impact the entire UC community.

Implementation

This three part initiative will be addressed in multiple phases.

The first part of this initiative will be achieved by establishing communication with UC Clermont Student Government and UC Blue Ash Tribunal presidency to gain understanding on current initiatives and needs for each student community.

The second phase will then be implemented through collaborative planning to set up the townhall for students, faculty, and staff to engage with one another as well as here to state of our campuses.

Lastly, to ensure longevity of relationships, we will work with UC administration to explore the development of social spaces on regional campuses to encourage social engagement.

Importance

Establishing a fund to provide financial relief for marginalized minority students on campus

Impact

The incorporation of the fund not only impacts the marginalized students facing financial hardship but the overall wellbeing and functionality of our university community for a problem for any student is a problem we should face together.

Implementation

Working with the Office of Ethnic Programs & Services, the African American Culture & Resource Center, and other relevant offices, we would establish criteria that would ensure the funding will benefit as many people as possible and ensure the longevity of this fund. From there, appropriate promotion of this funding option would be made available to staff and students and a process would be created to protect the confidentiality of the students in need.

Importance

Collaborating with University Housing and the LGBTQ+ Center to create a living-learning community to provide a safe, affirming and gender-inclusive experience with connections to staff and other resources.

Impact

This initiative directly impacts our LGBTQ+ student community by providing them a safe space to build community and encourages a more gender-inclusive community on campus.

Implementation

We plan to implement this initiative by working with the LGBTQ+ center and other LGBTQ+ identity organizations to draft a proposal that outlines the purpose and structure for this community. From there, this collaborative proposal will be sent to University Housing to finalize additional details needed to establish this learning community for the school year to follow.

Community

Importance

Increasing our engagement in campus and local philanthropy, Undergraduate Student Government will commit to having our members complete 150 hours of service to the community we reside in.

Impact

This initiative goes beyond the impacting our students and pours philanthropic efforts back into the surrounding Clifton and Cincinnati community.

Implementation

We plan to expand our relationship with the Center for Community Engagement and create various opportunity throughout the year for SG members to give back to community. In addition, we will explore partnerships with out volunteer student organizations on campus for collaboration on larger service events.

Spirit

Aligning to the CPS Strong initiative of President Pinto’s Next Lives Here vision, Undergraduate Student Government will partner with various organizations to engage students in a potential future at UC.

Following a historic season of multiple achievements for UC Athletics, we plan to partner with Athletics and other spirit organizations in establishing a new tradition to add to the celebration on game day.

This two-part initiative surrounds uplifting and celebrating the work our Black student-athletes achieve on and off the field. This includes “Melanin in Sports”, a media series highlighting their amazing contributions, and a program series that goes “The Extra M.I.L.E” in preparing and supporting our Black talent.
